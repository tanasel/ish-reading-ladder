How it works

← Back to the tool

One text, every level — in five steps.

Reading Ladder takes a text you already have and turns it into a simplified version for each EAL phase in your class — each with key words, comprehension questions and sentence starters. It does not use any AI of its own: it writes a careful prompt, you run it through ISH's approved AI, and the tool turns the reply into ready-to-print worksheets.

The five steps

1

Paste your text

Give it a title and subject, then paste the passage you want to differentiate. Tip: use your own writing or a public-domain text — that way nobody's copyright is involved. Leave the box empty to have the AI write a fresh text on the topic instead.

2

Choose the levels

Tick the EAL phases you need (two or three is usually plenty). Each shows its CEFR equivalent, and you can rename any label to match how your team places students. Add the "Original" level for your strongest readers or for comparison.

3

Pick what to include

The simplified passage is always included. Add a short summary, a key-word glossary, comprehension questions and sentence starters with one tap each. "More options" lets you set how many of each, and add a home-language glossary (e.g. Ukrainian, Arabic) if that helps your students. Then press Write the AI instructions.

4

Copy the instructions into your AI

The tool writes one set of instructions that already contains your text. Press Copy instructions, open Gemini (the AI ISH gives you through Google Workspace — sign in with your ISH account), paste and send. The AI answers with a long block that looks like computer code, full of { } brackets — that is exactly right, don't worry. Select all of it (Ctrl/Cmd + A) and copy.

5

Paste the reply back

Paste the whole reply into step 5 — even if the AI added a sentence like "Sure, here you go" — and press Build the worksheets. Stray text, code fences and "smart quotes" are cleaned up automatically. Your reading ladder appears, ready to read, copy, print or save as a PDF.

Getting the best results

  • Keep texts a sensible length. A few paragraphs work better than a whole chapter.
  • Two or three levels usually covers a class. You can always run it again for more.
  • Read the lowest level first. If a sentence is still too hard, ask the AI in the same chat to "make Phase 1 even simpler" and paste the new reply back.
  • Printing student copies? In the results, click Hide answer keys first — then Print / Save PDF gives clean worksheets without the answers. Each level prints on its own page.
  • Into Google Docs or Classroom? Use Copy all (or Copy on one level) to paste plain text you can format however you like.

Why this is GDPR & EU AI-Act safe

🔒 The student text never leaves your browser through this tool.

  • There is no account, no server and no database. Reading Ladder is a few static files.
  • The tool itself uploads nothing. It only builds text on your screen.
  • You decide when to copy the prompt into the AI ISH already provides. Best of all, use Gemini signed into your ISH Google account — the text then stays inside the school's Google Workspace, which ISH already has a data-processing agreement for. No new third-party processor to assess.
  • Because you paste your own or public-domain text, you are not sending someone else's copyrighted work to a service either.

This is the difference from a product like Diffit, where the text is sent to the vendor's own servers for processing — which is what makes that route hard to clear under GDPR and the EU AI Act. Always follow ISH's data-protection policy and only paste student-appropriate material into approved tools. If in doubt, check with the school's data-protection lead.

Common questions

Which AI do I use?

Gemini, signed in with your ISH Google account (the same Google Workspace login you use for email and Drive). That keeps everything inside the school's own Google account.

We already have MagicSchool — why this?

MagicSchool is great for many tasks, but it doesn't reliably take one text and rewrite it into several matched EAL phases — which is the one job this tool is built around. Use whichever suits the task.

Does it cost anything?

No. It is free static hosting and uses the AI your school already provides.

Is my text saved anywhere?

Only in your own browser, so you don't lose it on refresh. Clearing your browser data removes it.

The reply wouldn't build — what now?

Make sure you copied the whole reply. If the AI added an apology or notes, that's fine — the tool strips them. If it still fails, ask the AI to "reply with only the JSON object, nothing else" and try again.
